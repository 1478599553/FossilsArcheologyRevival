The quagga, whose name is 
onomatopoeia for the sound it made,
is an extinct horse that was closely
related to the zebra. It was 
partially covered by brown and white
stripes, with a brown body and white
legs. It was two and a half meters
long and almost a meter and a half
tall at the shoulder. Quaggas were 
lively equids, but unfortunately 
they were easy to kill, which lead 
to their extinction at the hands of 
humans in 1878. They were prized 
for their meat and their skins, 
which were used for trading.
